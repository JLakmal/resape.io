Steps to Making Cashew Curry

1. Soak the raw cashew nuts in water for about 2-3 hours. Drain the water and set the cashew nuts aside.

2. Heat vegetable oil in a large pan or pot over medium heat. Add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, fenugreek seeds, curry leaves, cinnamon stick, cardamom pods, and cloves. Let the spices sizzle and release their aroma for a minute.

3. Add chopped onions to the pan and saut√© until they turn golden brown.

4. Stir in the minced garlic, grated ginger, and sliced green chilies. Cook for another couple of minutes until the raw smell disappears.

5. Add turmeric powder and Sri Lankan curry powder (or the spice mix) to the pan. Stir well to combine all the spices with the onion mixture.

6. Add the soaked cashew nuts to the pan and mix them with the spice-onion mixture.

7. Pour in the coconut milk and add salt according to your taste. Stir everything together and bring it to a gentle simmer.

8. Lower the heat and let the cashew curry simmer for about 15-20 minutes until the cashews are cooked through and the sauce thickens to your desired consistency. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking and to ensure even cooking.

9. Once the cashews are tender and the curry reaches the desired thickness, remove it from heat.

10. Garnish with chopped cilantro (if using) and serve Sri Lankan Cashew Curry hot with rice, roti, or bread.
